What we're building

UpperBooks flags what needs a decision — categorization, missing accounts, compliance risk. Checked the same way, every time. Nothing changes until you approve it.

UpperBooks handles
AI suggests a category for every transaction on your synced copy in UpperBooks, with a confidence level based on how this client has categorized similar transactions before. It shows its reasoning up front — your bookkeeper agrees, or rejects and corrects it, teaching the AI for next time. The client reviews the batch and approves before anything writes back to QuickBooks.
Every transaction is checked against current IRS guidance in the background. Nothing needs your attention unless something's actually at risk — it's monitoring, not another inbox to manage.
UpperBooks proposes three kinds of changes: chart of accounts updates, checked against the industry template your practice wrote; vendor cleanup, like recognizing "Amzz" as Amazon; and new categorization rules, learned from patterns in past transactions. Every proposal is accept or decline — nothing is added on its own.
Sound, standard bookkeeping habits are checked automatically as transactions come in — the same way, for every client, every time. Nothing depends on remembering to check.
Vendor bills are checked for issues like possible duplicates before they're paid. UpperBooks flags what looks off and explains why — the user confirms and resolves it.
Left to your accounting software
UpperBooks reviews and suggests — it doesn't replace the record your accounting software keeps. Every category, account, and flag traces back to what's already there.
Once you agree to a categorization, UpperBooks posts it back to your accounting software. Reconciled transactions are never touched — even if UpperBooks would have categorized them differently.
Running reports and closing the books stays entirely in your accounting software. UpperBooks feeds it cleaner categorization along the way — it doesn't run the close itself.
If a habit, tool, or process already works for your practice, UpperBooks isn't there to change it. It fits around what you do, not the other way around.
How it works
STEP 1

Data arrives

Transactions, the chart of accounts, and past history sync in automatically — so the AI learns this client's patterns from day one.

STEP 2

AI analyzes

Checked against IRS guidance, your chart of accounts, and bookkeeping best practices — flagging what needs a decision.

STEP 3

Your bookkeeper decides

They agree, or reject and correct it — teaching the AI for next time. Every decision is saved to a batch.

STEP 4

The client approves

They review the batch and approve it. Only then does it write back to your accounting software.

Reconciled transactions are never touched, even after approval.

One shared workspace

Comment, resolve, and follow what matters — the whole history stays with the client, not scattered across email threads.

Clients are part of the conversation too — not locked out of it.
